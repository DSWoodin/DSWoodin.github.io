Research interests

Our research focus on developing algorithms and tools to help understand cancer immunology and immunotherapy, cell signaling, and gene expression regulation. We are developing a number of algorithms and tools to analyze high throughput genomics data, including RNA-seq, WES, CRISPR Screen, single cell, spatial transcriptomics, ATAC-seq data, etc. Through data integration and algorithm development, we try to model the specificity and function in tumor development, progression, drug response and resistance. When data lead us to unexpected but interesting results, we also design experiments on the bench hoping to understand the underlying mechanisms.

Cancer Immunology

We leverage the fast-growing volume of public omics/clinical data to identify novel gene targets/pathways involved in immunotherapy response/resistance. We also develop machine learning tools to identify key genes/pathways involved in cell signaling.

CRISPR screens

We are developing tools to integrate CRISPR-screen and other functional omics data to identify regulators of immune related phenotypes. We also develop CRISPR screen platforms to identify genes with dual roles in both cancer and immune cells.

Single cell

We are developing statistical methods to handle the increasing data complexity in single-cell research and further explore the underappreciated biological factors in antitumor immunity. We are unraveling the regulatory network with tumor microenvironment and identifying key genes that regulate the interactions between different cells in tumor microenvironment. We are also developing novel computational algorithms for both single cell and spatial transcriptomic data analyses.
